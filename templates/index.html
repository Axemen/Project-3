Whiskey

defining the product

Whisky is a distilled alcoholic beverage made from a fermented grain mash. Whisky are aged in wooden casks (barrels) and must have a minimum of alcohol by volume (ABV) of 40%. Whiskey can be made from a variety of these different grains including; corn, rye, barley, wheat, sorghum. The type and percentage of grains used in the distillation mash that determine the style/type of whiskey, e.g., single malt scotch or Rye Whiskey. Flavor by the types and amounts of grains used, the distillation process, the age (how long the whiskey ages is its cask) and type of cask used. Whiskey can be defined and labeled many ways and some styles must meet strict definitions.

The Data

scraping and cleaning

To begin this project, we sought out a valid source for whiskey reviews, one that not only held a comprehenisve and diverse catalog of whiskys but also supplied information on each in a consistent manner. After searching through a few options, we settled on using Whisky Advocate as the main data source to collect 4755 records for the following features: whiskey name, distillery, label, year, age, ABV, country of origin, style, category, review points and description.

Data was cleaned remove whiskeys with limited or suspect data, primarily those without prices. Price outliers of the top and bottom 5% were also removed. Each whiskey was also categorized into non-exclusive groupings of 16 varietys: Blended Malt Scotch Whisky, Blended Scotch Whisky, Blended Whiskey (Multi-country), Bourbon, Canada, Craft Whisky, Flavored Whiskey and Liqueurs, Generic Whisky (Multi-country), Irish, Japan, Redefined Style, Rye Whisky, Single Grain Whisky (Multi-country), Single Malt Scotch, Single Malt Whisky (Multi-country), and White Whisky. A total of 4726 records remained after cleaning.

Price Versus Age

does market value increase with age?

The clean data had 4,726 records with whiskeys from 28 countries. Prices ranged from $2 to $157,000 with ages (timein casks) from 1 year to 70 years. Whiskeys in the top and bottom 5% of pricing were dropped as outliers, so file price range was from $25 to $625. A Box-and-Whisker chart of the entire data set showed a wide distribution between whiskeys age and price.


Whiskey Popularity

what sells?

Single Malt Scotch is the most popular whiskey distilled in the world. Three times more types of the Single Malt has been distilled as compared to second most popular whiskey, bourbon. Additionally ,more distillers are distilling Single Malt Scotch than any other style of whiskey.


What Do Critics Know

what sells?

The rating scores from whiskey critics were correlated with the prices listed on the Whiskey Advocates web page. For all 16 styles of whiskey, no predictive correlation could be found between the critics score and price. Some bargain priced whiskeys scored very high and vise versa.


Single malt is special

predictive pricing

Along with being the most popular, Single Malt Scotch has the most predicable pricing based on its age, time in the cask. A Single Malt Scotch will increase on average $11 for every additional year in the cask. For example, a 15-year-old Single Malt should cost roughly $33 more than a 12-year-old Single Malt of the same type: 3 years x $11/year = $33.
